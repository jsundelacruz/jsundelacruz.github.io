What is it and who is it for?

TransLingua is a social language-learning app for people who have just moved to a city with a foreign language, it uses the environment to add context when learning words and phrases.

Research

Interviews and Personas

Through informal interviews, asking people what they currently used to translate text in another language, how they accustomed themselves to foreign cities, and what language-learning tools they used, I created personas to synthesize the needs and trouble people were having.

What do people use to learn a language or translate text right now?
Language module lessons
  • Basics to advanced lessons.
  • Translate pages on the web.
  • Flashcard-style memorization.
  • Link with images, videos, etc.
  • Gamified process.
Examples: DuoLingo, Memrise
Text translation and travel tools/tips

  • Common words/phrase.
  • Text translations over the web.
  • Save translations.
  • Option to hear translation.
  • Spoken or written input.

Examples: Google Translate, iTranslate

Photo-translation apps
  • Doesn’t require typing in text.
  • Optical character recognition.
  • Best used for clear typed writing.
  • Internet connected not required.
  • Option to hear translation.
Examples: Word Lens, Bing Translator

People are constantly seeing text as they move throughout a city. Here are examples of text often seen on a daily basis.
City signs
Restaurants and bars
Printed media
Product packaging

TransLingua is meant to complement foundational language-learning tools. It takes photo-translation software a step further by saving the images, building a city archive. By doing so, it strives to create memories based on images commonly seen in your environment and creates discussion amongst the community of learners.

How can we add context to language people are learning...
...using real-world examples to familiarize them with what they see in their surroundings?
